- You require indemnification, warranty, or custom support terms

## What Does AGPL-3.0 Require?

AGPL-3.0 is a strong copyleft license. Key requirements:
- If you modify PyClaw, you must release your modifications under AGPL-3.0
- If you provide PyClaw's functionality over a network (SaaS), you must provide the complete source code of your modified version to all users of the service
- Any software that links to or integrates with PyClaw must also be released under AGPL-3.0 (this is the "network copyleft" provision)

## FAQ

**Q: I want to build a product on top of PyClaw and sell it. Which license?**
A: If your product is open-sourced under AGPL-3.0, you can use the free AGPL license. Otherwise, you need a commercial license.

**Q: I run PyClaw internally at my company — do I need a commercial license?**
A: No. Internal use within your organization (no external users) does not trigger AGPL's network service clause.

**Q: Can I contribute to PyClaw if I'm using it under AGPL?**
A: Yes. All contributors retain copyright in their contributions. By contributing, you agree your contributions are licensed under AGPL-3.0 and can be relicensed by the maintainers under the commercial license.

**Q: What if I use PyClaw but don't modify it?**
A: If you provide it as a service (SaaS), AGPL still requires you to offer users the complete source code. If you only use it internally without external users, there's no obligation.
